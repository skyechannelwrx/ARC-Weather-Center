## Operational Summary
**...HIGH HEAT RISK...**

A high heat risk is seeming very likely as we approach Field Day. Those operating outdoors between the hours of 11am to 5pm, on the 26th, 27th, and 28th, should be aware of the potential for extreme heat. High heat index values of up to 102 are possible. Heat index values could go higher; the potential exists for heat index values up to 105, which would warrant the issuance of a Moderate risk.

Please refer to the Mitigation section for safety information.

## Mitigation
**...HIGH HEAT RISK PREPAREDNESS...**

High heat index values of up to 102, and possibly up to 105, are possible on the 26th, 27th, and 28th. These heat index values are hazardous, and could result in heat exshaustion or heat stroke if proper care is not exercised during the peak heating hours, which are between 11am to 5pm. 

Wear light, loose-fitting clothing. Drink plenty of water. Stay out of the sun when possible, and avoid strenuous activity through the peak heating hours of the day. Wear light colored clothing, dark colored clothing may absorb more heat from the sun. Return to an air conditioned room or vehicle if the heat becomes unbearable.

## Discussion
**...HIGH HEAT RISK...**

The high heat risk threat for Field Day has prompted the issuance of a Low risk. This low risk is for hazardous heat index values up to 102. However, it is possible heat index values could exceed this, and reach up to 105. If this is the case, a Moderate risk would be required for a Moderate hazard. Even if a Moderate risk is issued, however, mitigation would only be the necessary requirement as it relates to this high heat risk. 

**...FOG AND CONDENSATION...**

Fog is becoming increasingly more likely, particularly over the night hours of the 27th and early morning hours of the 28th. Visibility could be drastically reduced. The ECWMF and the medium-range NAM model, a model which was previously out of range, both highlight our area within a lowered visibility section. More time will be necessary to determine if this threat is to materialize, and to what extent it will. Currently visibility reduction estimates are 4 to 2 miles visibility. However, the RRFS currently depicts widespread reductions in visibility, currently across the night hours of the 26th. This includes small pockets of reduced visibility to 1.5 to 1 mile or less in areas, with our area mostly being impacted by a reduction around 1.5 miles. 

The RRFS particularly appears to support a condensation risk already across the 26th into the 27th, but until the full range can be viewed, nothing as of yet is conclusive.

**...THUNDERSTORMS AND RAIN...**

Thunderstorms and assosciated precipitation may be possible on the 26th, 27th, and 28th. The NAM has displayed CAPE values as high as 3000 J/Kg in the early morning hours of the 26th. CAPE values will not drastically reduce overnight, and storms may be able to continue convecting through the overnight period. In any case, precipitation is seeming the most likely threat at this time, but to amount of rain will occur is yet to be determined. Unpredictability in storm placement and timing makes this task considerably harder, and thus, a low risk assosciated with rainfall was ommitted. Thunderstorm likelihood cannot yet be ascertained, and thus, a Moderate risk based on Thunderstorms was ommitted. If certainty can be gained in the likelihood and probability of Thunderstorms, a Moderate risk could be issued based on this threat. Depending on different models, a Moderate risk for thunderstorms and a subsequent Moderate risk for precipitation could be added in the future.

**...WIND THREAT...**

The RRFS displayed areas of increased wind gusts up to 20 MPH, and even some areas of up to 30 MPH. However, this appears isolated, and may coincide with the model depicting thunderstorms. The ECWMF also displayed pockets of increased winds. If this is related to thunderstorms, then no wind threat will be added, but will be marked alongside the thunderstorm threat for the area, should one materialize.

The next debrief will be issued by 22Z on June 24th.
