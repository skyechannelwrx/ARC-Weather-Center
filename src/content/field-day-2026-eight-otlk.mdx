## Operational Summary
**...HIGH HEAT RISK...**

A high heat risk is  likely during Field Day. Those operating outdoors between the hours of 11am to 5pm, on the 26th, 27th, and 28th, should be aware of the potential for extreme heat. High heat index values of up to 105 are possible. Although unpredictability exists in what the actual Heat Index will be, the chance for it to reach 102 or higher is present, and thus, increases the heat risk. 

**...ISOLATED TO SCATTERED THUNDERSTORMS...**

Thunderstorms may be present on the 26th and 27th, especially during the afternoon to evening hours, which may pose a threat to equipment and life if not properly mitigated. These thunderstorms will likely produce lightning. Although it is not guaranteed we will see thunderstorms, the probability is high enough that one or two thunderstorms being present cannot be ruled out.

Please refer to the Mitigation section for safety information.

## Mitigation
**...HIGH HEAT RISK PREPAREDNESS...**

High heat index values of up to 105 are possible on the 26th, 27th, and 28th. These heat index values are hazardous, and could result in heat exshaustion or heat stroke if proper care is not exercised during the peak heating hours, which are between 11am to 5pm. 

Wear light, loose-fitting clothing. Drink plenty of water. Stay out of the sun when possible, and avoid strenuous activity through the peak heating hours of the day. Wear light colored clothing, dark colored clothing may absorb more heat from the sun. Return to an air conditioned room or vehicle if the heat becomes unbearable.

**...THUNDERSTORM PREPAREDNESS...**

Be ready to move indoors quickly. Be aware of the weather, check the skies frequently and monitor weather radar. Listen to NOAA weather radio for any Special Weather Statements or Severe Thunderstorm Warnings which may be issued by the National Weather Service. Monitor the weather, and be on the ready in case you must act to protect your equipment or life. 

Remember: when thunder roars, go indoors!
