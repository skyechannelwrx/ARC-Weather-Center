## Summary

In the short-term, conditions appear unfavorable for conducting outdoor activity, particularly due to an enhanced risk of heavy precipitation and a higher risk of precipitation in general for practically the entire state of Florida. This will likely contribute to Moderate to High outdoor operational hazard risks for the short-term, should we wish to conduct any outdoor activity during that period.

As a result, it may be best to wait for this period to pass.

## Discussion 

Medium-range plus short-range models and trends are favoring precipitation chances above normal for almost the entire Florida peninsula through May 27th, today, throught to approximately June 7th, or longer. These high precipitation chances carry with them chances for heavy and possibly flooding rainfall, which may increase our Operational Hazard Index to Moderate or High, depending on how conditions play out. 

The Climate Prediction Center has outlined Florida as having a moderate risk (between 40% to 60% chance for heavy precipitation) only a few miles south of our location and preferred operation spots. However, we are also under a slight risk, between 20% to 40% chance for precipitation, making conditions even less favorable for outdoor conditions.

These precipitation chances seem to apply for almost the entire day, and no particular difference between early morning, to afternoon, to evening, and to night hours can be made, except that some days will favor rain earlier than normal. Development for thunderstorms within this heavy precipitation chance is also possible, and will further discourage outdoor activity.

Opportunity for outdoor activites may arise again when the dust from Africa traveling across the Atlantic intrudues upon tropical moisture, which will inhibit precipitation chances, or work to lower them and make storms generally unfavorable. Humidity during this time should, theoretically, become less if any substantial dust intrusion can occur.
