## Bulletin
* **Above normal confidence in increased impact likelihood.**

* A strong to very strong El Nino could occur.

* The development of El Nino may bring significant weather and climate impacts to the Florida peninsula, which may affect operations.

## Summary
Above normal confidence in increased impact likelihood for Winter Field Day 2027.

Over the past two months, conditions have been continously monitored in advance of Winter Field Day, as well as in preparation for Field Day 2026. While conducting monitoring of weather setup and conditions over the past two months, an increasing trend in El Nino favorability has been denoted as we advance toward the later half of this year. Currently, it is anticipated we are in a weak El Nino and currently moving toward a moderate El Nino. Current Climate Prediction Center trends favor a weak El Nino this month in June with the chance for an emergence of a moderate El Nino by July. 

Further, CPC El Nino strength probabilities have begun to favor a strong El Nino, which is Pacific temperature anomalies greater than or equal to 1.5 degrees celsisus and less than 2 degrees celsius, to a very strong El Nino, which is Pacific temperature anomalies greater than or equal to 2 degrees celsisus. The climate imapcts from such an event will be felt across the world, and due to the intensity of them, potentially significant impacts could occur to Florida. 

Should the El Nino develop into a strong or very strong El Nino, it is predicted the development of the subtropical jet may occur, where the jetstream develops further south and remains in that position for the duration of the El Nino. Currently, the 2027 spring predictability barrier prevents any certainty in how long this El Nino will last, but if it's development is completed by the end of this year, it may, possibly, continue into the summer of next year. 

The El Nino will favor cooler temperatures across the Florida peninsula and more rainfall. Rainfall and potentially thunderstorms could become a hazard during Winter Field Day in 2027.
