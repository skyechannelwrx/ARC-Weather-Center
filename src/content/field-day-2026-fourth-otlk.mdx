## Operational Summary
Operations are still within comfortable parameters, but the addition of a slight excessive heat risk for the Florida peninsula through the days we intend to operate was issued by the CPC. Threats are appearing more defined; but due to being more than 7 days out, confidence in timing and occurrence remains low. A heat risk is appearing more likely.

## Mitigation
No mitigation action is needed at this time.

## Discussion
The GFS and ECWMF models have begun to agree more on a defined heat risk for our area, and heat index temperatures may even exceed the 102 barrier, or get close to it. If this is to occur, a Moderate hazard index will be required. As this is the case, an increase in the probability of a moderate risk was added, and the confidence in a low risk being issued has increased. 

This heat risk is present across much of the Southeast. Unless a cold front or tropical system is to move through during our time of operations, it is unlikely that we will see any significant relief from the temperatures. It is not likely for the temperatures to reach or exceed 110 degrees, as of current. 

Comparing the wind gusts for both the GFS, GEFS, and ENS, it is still too early to determine if a wind threat may exist. If a wind threat does exist, this threat will be low, and the wind gusts are not expected to exceed 30 MPH. The current estimated wind gusts are expected to sit between 20 to 15 mph. 20 mph is the limit at which a low risk will be issued.

Rain and thunderstorm threat cannot be ruled out. 

However, the likelihood of condensation and/or fog is increasingly minimal, and thus, the risk for fog and dewpoint is likely very limited, and the likelihood of a low risk issuance based upon it is not likely. Temperatures and dewpoints have been concentrated more on the upper end for both models, and the dewpoints remain out of the 5 degree spread required for fog to occur. 

Based upon current guidance, the issuance of a low risk by the next outlook may be required if conditions deteriorate. 

The next outlook will be issued on June 21st, by 12:00 PM Eastern Time.
